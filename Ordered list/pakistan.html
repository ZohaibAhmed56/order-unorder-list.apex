MINAR-E-PAKISTAN

Minar-e-Pakistan is a national monument located in Lahore, Pakistan. It was built to commemorate the Lahore Resolution, which was passed on March 23, 1940, and marked the beginning of the Pakistan Movement. The monument is a 62-meter-tall (203 feet) minaret that is shaped like a flower. It is made of concrete and is decorated with intricate designs and calligraphy. The monument is surrounded by a large park, which is a popular spot for recreation and relaxation. Minar-e-Pakistan is not only a symbol of Pakistan's independence but also a reminder of the country's rich history and cultural heritage. It is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in learning about Pakistan's past and its struggle for independence.
